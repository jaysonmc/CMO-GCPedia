Recruitment

What is Recruitment at the Office of the Chief Information Officer?

Several organizations signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) with the Office of the Chief Information Officer (OCIO). As part of this MOU, we are responsible to conduct recruitment on behalf of the IM/IT community.

Pools and processes

Our pools have:

  • New candidates added on an ongoing basis
  • No expiry date
  • No assigned group and level

Candidate Assessment

Following a needs analysis with the community, the following competencies were identified for assessment:

  • Analytical thinking
  • Client focus
  • Teamwork
  • Communication

We asses these competencies through three steps:

  1. Group interview
  2. Self-assessment
  3. Reference check

Step 1 - Group Interview

The group interview concept was validated by the Personnel Psychology Centre at the Public Service Commission.

The group interview:

  • Consists of 4 candidates
  • Includes 12 situational questions
  • Lasts up to 2.5 hours

Board members are OCIO employees and volunteers from the community.

To move forward in the process, candidates must obtain a 60% pass mark for each competency during the interview.

Step 2 ‚Äì Self-assessment

Candidates successful in the interview move on to the self-assessment step.

The self-assessment:

  • Defines the 3 competencies assessed: analytical thinking, client focus and teamwork
  • Instructs candidates to provide examples of how they have demonstrated the 3 competencies
  • Requires candidates to include the name of 2 referees per competency that can validate their examples

Step 3 ‚Äì Reference check

At this step, we send a reference check form to the candidate‚Äôs referees to validate the examples provided by the candidate and to answer other reference check questions.

Additional Assessment

Before proceeding to hire a candidate, we recommend having a right fit meeting. If an organization determines additional testing is required, it is possible and up to the organization to complete the assessment. For that reason, it is the hiring organization that determines whether they consider our pool as fully or partially assessed.

The Hiring Process

Organizations that are interested in hiring from our pool are provided with the candidate‚Äôs:

  • Application
  • Resume
  • Self-assessment
  • References
  • Interview results

Offers

Typically, positions offered through our pool are term or indeterminate, throughout Canada (mostly in the National Capital Region) and range from CS-01 to CS-04.

The Statement of Merit used to obtain priority clearance is in some instances modified to include experience criteria from the candidate answers to the asset questions in the application and from resumes. It is up to hiring organizations to determine the appropriate position level for a candidate as there was no group and level on the job posters.

Once a candidate is hired, a copy of the signed letter of offer needs to be sent to us.

ConneCSion

ConneCSion is a recruitment event that we hold a few times a year for candidates in our pool and hiring managers who are looking for talented candidates.

The format

We are always working towards making ConneCSion as efficient and successful as possible. Based on experience and feedback, we found that a combination of networking and scheduled interviews works very well. This means having an open space designated to networking and small breakout rooms designated to scheduled interviews.

Note: because of COVID-19, our most recent event was fully virtual.
